01 · Diagnosis

The honest read of where things stand.

Before proposing tactics, here's the diagnosis we'd defend in a working session. Each of these is solvable — and each one shows up in pipeline within the first two quarters once it is.

01

Fragmented growth architecture

Assets live across www, get, try, and insights subdomains. They aren't acting as one revenue engine yet — methodology is strong, but the path from a researching buyer to a booked demo is uneven and inconsistently instrumented.

SymptomPages don't compound
02

Buyers research where you're not

Enterprise marketers self-educate across Google, AI engines, YouTube, LinkedIn, review sites, and comparison articles. Competitors like Zappi and Suzy have built ecosystems across all of these. Swayable is under-represented in most.

SymptomMissing from shortlists
03

The proof isn't packaged

Swayable's proprietary data — RCT studies, brand-lift benchmarks, the influencer report — is the most defensible content moat in the category. Today it sits as PDFs and one-off posts rather than a connected SEO, PR, and sales-enablement system.

SymptomAsset, not engine
02 · Approach

Five workstreams, run by one team, tied to one outcome.

Each pillar is independently measurable, but they reinforce each other. We don't believe in running SEO without CRO, or content without distribution, or paid without organic learnings flowing back into the page system.

01
Search architecture

Build the revenue search architecture across www, get, try, and insights.

Treat all four properties as one system. Map every page to buyer intent, fix the technical foundation, and ship a deliberate set of commercial, methodology, use-case, and comparison pages.

  • Crawl + intent map across every property
  • Technical: speed, schema, canonicalization, internal links
  • Commercial pages: creative testing, brand lift, message testing, audience testing
  • Use-case pages by buyer pain (CPG, agencies, political, entertainment, retail)
OutcomeCoherent funnel from research to demo
02
Competitor intercept

Capture buyers already comparing Zappi, Suzy, Kantar, and Qualtrics.

The fastest path to pipeline. Honest comparison pages — not hit pieces — built around where each competitor is genuinely strong and where Swayable's RCT methodology wins.

  • Vendor-vs pages: Swayable vs Zappi, Suzy, Kantar LINK AI, Qualtrics
  • Category pages: best creative testing platforms, best brand-lift tools
  • "Alternatives" pages: Zappi alternatives, Suzy alternatives
  • Each page paired with proof, methodology snippet, and a demo path
OutcomeIn every shortlist
03
Authority & AI visibility

Become the answer when buyers — and the AI advising them — validate vendors.

AI engines and B2B buyers both lean on third-party validation: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, analyst notes, listicles, and PR. We treat reviews as a campaign and pitch into category articles deliberately.

  • Claim and clean up directory profiles (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Greenbook)
  • Compliant customer review campaign with happy enterprise accounts
  • Pitch inclusion into "best of" listicles and category articles
  • Track AI answer share-of-voice on a defined prompt set
OutcomeValidated wherever buyers look
04
Proprietary data moat

Turn your test library into a moat competitors can't replicate.

RCT studies, brand-lift benchmarks, and the influencer report are content competitors structurally cannot match. We package them into a connected SEO + PR + LinkedIn + video + sales system.

  • OCR + structured extraction pipeline for existing studies
  • Pillar pages on persuasion benchmarks by industry and channel
  • Repurpose into LinkedIn, YouTube, webinar, PR, and sales collateral
  • Light interactive tools: media-waste calculator, RCT explainer, brief generator
OutcomeDefensible category authority
05
CRO, paid validation & reporting

Wire everything to pipeline — not traffic, not rankings.

Paid search isn't a separate upsell; it's the research engine that tells SEO which messages actually convert. Reporting is built around demo demand and pipeline, not vanity metrics. Without this layer, the rest of the work isn't auditable — so it's not optional.

  • GA4, GTM, Search Console, Looker Studio dashboard
  • HubSpot/Salesforce attribution wired to web conversions
  • Small exact-match PPC tests on high-intent terms — feed learnings back to SEO
  • Page-level conversion tests on commercial and comparison pages
  • Demo-form flow audit and friction reduction
  • Monthly AI Visibility Scorecard against a defined prompt set
OutcomeEvery dollar traceable to pipeline

05 · Reporting

We won't report SEO activity. We'll report buyer discovery and pipeline movement.

If a metric doesn't connect to a demo request, it doesn't go on the dashboard. The reporting stack is set up in the first 30 days so that every subsequent decision — what to invest in, what to cut — is grounded in the same numbers your sales team already trusts.

Stack: Google Search Console, GA4, GTM, a Looker Studio dashboard wired to HubSpot/Salesforce, plus a monthly AI Visibility Scorecard tracked against a defined prompt set.
